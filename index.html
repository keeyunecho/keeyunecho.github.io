About Me

Who am I?

> > > < < <

Hello!

My name is Kelly Cho, and I'm a computer science major at the University of California, Berkeley, graduating Spring 2017.

Now that I'm a senior I've had the opportunity to take a wide variety of the CS courses Berkeley has to offer, but computer graphics is the class where I remember sitting in lecture one day and being overcome with a feeling of conviction that yes, this is what I came to college to learn (it was a lecture on the rendering equation!). The field of computer graphics creates cutting edge technology that is the backbone of visuals that range from "I can't believe that explosion was CG" to "I can't believe I'm crying over an animated cowboy," and I am very glad to be a part of that.

Summer 2016 I was a software engineering intern at Penrose Studios, a VR/AR animation studio, where I worked on a tool to enable artists to draw and storyboard in virtual reality. Creating my own art is rewarding, but I think creating tools that allow other people to create art was equally or in some ways even more rewarding. It means the work I do is part of something bigger, which is why I started studying computer science in the first place.

When I'm not in front of the computer I enjoy playing guitar, reading (currently burning through the Mistborn series), and tracking down the best waffles in the Bay Area. I also enjoy hiking, but only whe I've had a reasonable amount of sleep.
